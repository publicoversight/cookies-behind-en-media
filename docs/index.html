Cookie Vendor Network

Third-party tracking domains behind 18 global news outlets

Every time you open a news article, dozens of companies you've never heard of receive a signal. This map is a small imperfect and uncomplete first step towards making that invisible network visible — showing 1,168 connections between 18 outlets and the ad tech companies embedded in their pages.

Limitations

1
Snapshot in time. Cookie inventories change week to week. This data reflects one crawl date.
2
No consent interaction. Vendors were captured on page arrival, before accepting or declining cookie banners.
3
Homepage bias. Some vendors only appear on specific content verticals and may be undercounted.
4
Domain presence ≠ data transfer. A request being made does not prove personal data was shared — only that contact occurred.
5
Coverage varies. Some outlets (BBC, Reuters, DW) have far fewer entries than others, reflecting access and crawl limitations rather than necessarily fewer trackers.

How to read the map

Each diamond ◆ is a news outlet. Each circle ● is a third-party vendor. Lines connect outlets to the vendors loaded on their pages. The bigger the circle, the more outlets that vendor appears on. Click any node to explore its connections.

Glossary

Cookie A small file stored in your browser by a website or third party, used to identify and track you across visits and sites.
Third-party domain A domain loaded by a page that belongs to a company other than the site you're visiting.
Ad Tracking Domains used by advertising networks to profile users, serve targeted ads, and measure campaign performance.
Analytics Tools that measure audience behaviour — page views, time on site, scroll depth — often storing user-level identifiers.
CDN Content Delivery Network — infrastructure that serves files (fonts, scripts, images) from servers near the user. Generally less privacy-invasive, but still logs request metadata.
DSP / SSP Demand-Side Platform / Supply-Side Platform — software that automates buying and selling of ad space in real time, passing user data between parties.
CMP Consent Management Platform — the cookie banner system. Manages user consent choices, though compliance varies widely in practice.
RTB Real-Time Bidding — an auction that happens in milliseconds when a page loads, during which your profile is shared with hundreds of advertisers.
